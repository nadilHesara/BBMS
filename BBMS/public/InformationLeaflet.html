Blood Donation Eligibility Guidelines

Ensuring Safe Blood Donation for Donors and Recipients
Important: These criteria are established to ensure both donor safety and the quality of donated blood for recipients.

Who Can Donate Blood?

To be accepted as a blood donor, individuals must fulfill several specific criteria designed to protect both the donor's health and ensure the safety of the blood supply.

Age Requirements

Must be above 18 years and below 60 years of age

Time Between Donations

At least 4 months must have elapsed since previous donation

Health Requirements

Must be free from any serious disease condition or pregnancy

Hemoglobin Level

Must be more than 12g/dL (tested prior to each donation)

  • Must have a valid identity card or document to prove identity
  • Must be free from "Risk Behaviors" (detailed below)

Risk Behaviors

Individuals with the following behaviors are not eligible to donate blood:

  • Homosexuals
  • Sex workers and their clients
  • Drug addicts
  • Engaging in sexual activity with any of the above groups
  • Having more than one sexual partner

For more information about blood donation, please consult with your local blood bank or healthcare provider.
